Punto.

Sia \(k\) un intero per cui la proprietà sia vera, cioè sia \(\sum_{i=0}^{k}i^{2}=\frac{k(k+1)(2k+1)}{6}\text{.}\) Allora

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=0}^{k+1}i^{2}\amp = \sum_{i=0}^{k}i^{2}+(k+1)^{2}=\frac{k(k+1)(2k+1)}{6}+(k+1)^{2}\\ \amp = \frac{k(k+1)(2k+1)+6(k+1)^{2}}{6}\\ \amp =\frac{(k+1)(k(2k+1)+6(k+1))}{6} \\ \amp = \frac{(k+1)((k+2)(2(k+1)+1))}{6} \end{align*}
